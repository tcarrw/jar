On the 1/7 Temporal Rhythm in 42‑Day Cycles

Updated 2025-09-11

We consider a 42‑day interval partitioned into six heptads. By fixing Day 1 at a full moon, each seventh day becomes a stable checkpoint (Full, Last Quarter, New, First Quarter, Full, …). This discretization reduces planning entropy and simplifies retrospective indexing (“Week k, Day d”).

Practical note: Map any date t to a cycle index via n = ⌊(t − start)/1 day⌋ + 1 and phase by n mod 7.
